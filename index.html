      Staff Onboarding Module

      Welcome to Azzurro Hotels!

      Module Purpose

      This onboarding module introduces new team members to:

      • How Azzurro Hotels operates.
      • Our self check-in accommodation model.
      • The difference between Front-of-House and Back-Office roles.
      • How teams work together to deliver a consistent guest experience

      By the end of this module, you should understand your role, your responsibilities, and how your work supports guests and other team members.

      1. About Azzurro Hotels

      Azzurro Hotels operates modern pod hostels and serviced accommodation across Australia. Our properties are designed for efficient, contactless stays, using technology and automation rather than traditional onsite reception desks.

      We operate using a self check-in (Airbnb-style) model, where:

      • Guests check in online before arrival
      • Access is provided via secure door codes
      • There is no physical front desk
      • Guest support is provided remotely by our virtual reception team

      This model allows us to offer flexibility to guests while maintaining consistent service standards across all properties.

      2. Our Operating Model (How Things Work)

      Key Principles

      • 24-hour self check-in for guests
      • Check-in time starts from 3:00 PM
      • Access codes activate from 3:00 PM onwards only
      • Guest communication is handled remotely
      • Onsite staff (e.g. cleaners) do not handle check-in

      Clear communication and system accuracy are critical to prevent guest confusion.

      3. Front-of-House Staff (Guest-Facing Roles)

      Front-of-House staff are responsible for direct guest communication and support, delivered remotely.

      Your Responsibilities

      • Respond to guest enquiries via:
        • SMS
        • WhatsApp
        • Email
        • Phone
      • Assist guests with:
        • Online check-in
        • Access codes
        • Room or bed details
        • General stay questions
      • Escalate issues when required, following SOPs

      What Success Looks Like

      • Guests understand the process before arrival
      • Fewer access-related complaints
      • Calm, clear, professional communication at all times

      You are the voice of Azzurro Hotels to our guests.

      4. Back-Office Staff (Operations & Systems Roles)

      Back-Office staff support the guest experience behind the scenes by managing systems, data, and processes.

      Your Responsibilities

      • Maintain booking and property systems
      • Manage and update:
        • Automated message templates
        • Check-in workflows
        • SOPs and training materials
      • Monitor integrations (reservations, messaging, access codes)
      • Ensure information used by Front-of-House staff is accurate and current
      • Support escalation and issue resolution

      What Success Looks Like

      • Systems run smoothly with minimal manual intervention
      • Messaging is clear, consistent, and approved
      • Front-of-House teams have the information they need to support guests confidently

      Back-Office work ensures consistency and reliability across all properties.

      5. How Teams Work Together

      At Azzurro Hotels, guest experience depends on strong alignment between teams

      • Back-Office builds and maintains systems
      • Front-of-House delivers the guest experience
      • SOPs ensure everyone follows the same standards

      When systems and communication are aligned, guests experience a smooth, stress-free stay.

      6. Key Expectations for All Staff

      All team members are expected to:

      • Follow approved SOPs and templates
      • Communicate clearly and professionally
      • Avoid giving guests conflicting information
      • Escalate issues rather than improvising
      • Maintain a consistent brand voice

      Consistency protects both guest experience and team efficiency.

      Introduction to the Tools

      Tools Overview – Introduction

      Welcome to the Tools Overview training.

      In this, we’ll walk through the key systems we use daily to manage reservations, guest access, communication, and security.

      These tools help ensure smooth operations, accurate information, and a safe experience for both guests and staff. Each system has a specific purpose, and understanding how they work together is essential to your role..

      Let’s get started.

      1. Cloudbeds

      Cloudbeds Training – System Overview

      Cloudbeds is our main central system.

      This is where all bookings from different channels, such as Booking.com and Agoda, are collected and managed in one place.

      Dashboard Overview

      Upon logging into Cloudbeds, this is the Dashboard.

      This page gives you a quick overview of:

      • Arrivals and departures
      • Current accommodations booked
      • Today’s activities
      • A list of guest reservations

      This helps you immediately see what’s happening for the day.

      Switching Properties

      To access different properties, click the property name located at the upper left corner of the page.

      A dropdown will appear showing all other available properties. Simply select the property you want to manage.

      The three horizontal bars beside the property name open the main menu, where you can access other Cloudbeds features and options.

      Calendar View

      On the upper right of the page, click the calendar icon to open the Cloudbeds Calendar.

      This is the most commonly used page during your shift.

      Since all bookings come from multiple channels, any availability shown here is automatically reflected across all connected booking platforms.

      The calendar uses color indicators to show reservation status:

      • Blue – Confirmed bookings
      • Green – Checked-in guests
      • Gray – Checked-out guests

      You can click the legend at the bottom left for reference.

      Viewing a Reservation

      When you click on a reservation, you’ll see full details including:

      • Guest name
      • Reservation status (confirmed, in-house, or checked-out)
      • Number of guests
      • Balance due
      • Reservation notes

      Always check the notes section for important instructions or reminders.

      Room Types and Rooms (Left Panel)

      On the left side, you’ll see the Room Types and individual room setup.

      Guests book a room type, and Cloudbeds automatically assigns one of the rooms under that category based on availability.

      Reservations List & Filter Page

      On the upper right, click the Reservations icon to open the Reservations List & Filter page.

      Here’s what you can do on this page:

      • Filter section (top):
        • Narrow reservations by booking date, stay dates, status, room type, booking source/channel, and meal plan.
      • Apply / Clear:
        • Apply your selected filters or reset them.
      • Reservations table (bottom):
        • Displays reservation number, guest name, booking date, property, room number, and check-in date.
      • Search bar:
        • Quickly find a reservation by name, reservation number, or keyword.
      • Bulk Action:
        • Perform actions on multiple reservations at once.
      • Pagination & view size:Pagination & view size:
        • Control how many reservations appear per page and navigate through results.

      This page is mainly used to search, review, filter, and manage reservations efficiently.

      Top-Right Utility Icons

      Finally, on the top-right corner, you’ll see the utility icons:

      • Bell icon – Notifications for reservations, tasks, and system alerts
      • Megaphone icon – Important Cloudbeds announcements and updates
      • Printer icon – Print tools for reservation lists and reports
      • Chat bubble icon – Messages or Inbox for guest and channel communications
      • User icon – Profile settings, preferences, permissions, and logout
      Closing Line

      Cloudbeds is the main system you’ll use throughout your shift, so becoming familiar with the dashboard, calendar, and reservations page is essential for smooth daily operations.

      2. Goki

      GOKI Training – System Overview

      Goki is our digital key system.

      It allows us to enable and disable access to rooms and entrances remotely. Goki is connected to CloudBeds, so once a booking is in the system, guests can access their rooms and entrances using a PIN code or the Goki app, depending on the setup. Guests who download the Goki app and activate their key can also open the doors directly from the app. When they are near the main entrance or their room, they can tap the digital key button in the app to unlock the door. Their access will only work during their stay dates and will automatically stop after check-out.

      Dashboard Overview

      First, open Goki in your browser and log in using your work email and password. After you log in, you’ll see the dashboard.

      Here, you can see:

      • Today’s check-ins and check-outs
      • Current in-house guests

        • and

      • Lock/battery status for rooms
        • Ideally, batteries lower than 40% should be changed to avoid access issues for guests.

      On the top left, you can change the property by clicking the dropdown and selecting the correct address. Always make sure you’re on the right property before checking reservations or sending keys.

      Messages

      Next, let’s look at Messages.

      • Messages allow us to communicate directly with guests through Goki.
      • Guests can only receive and send messages if they have the Goki app and an account.
      • If a guest does not have the app or an account, Goki will not give you the option to message them.

        • Unread Goki messages are marked in red.

      • Once a message is opened, it will lose the red mark.
      • Always make sure to respond to messages once you open them, so nothing is left unattended. It is mandatory to monitor messages throughout your shift.
      Shoutouts

      Shoutoutis Goki’s message blast feature.

      • We use Shoutouts to inform guests in bulk, like a notice board.
      • You can send mass messagesv to:
        • All guests in the property
        • Guests in specific rooms
        • Guests in a specific property only

      This is useful for announcements like quiet hours, maintenance notices, or events.

      Activities

      Under Activities, we post any activities happening in the property and invite guests to sign up.

      A common example is our complimentary dinner sign-up.

      • We create an activity in Goki.
      • Guests can sign up through the app.
      • Those who sign up will appear in the list.

      By around 4 p.m., we check the list and make sure everyone who signed up is added to the system or prepared for the activity.

      Reservations

      Now, let’s go to the Reservations section.

      Here, you can:

      • Check guest reservation details
      • See the booking status
      • View their PIN code for Goki access.

      You’ll also see dot indicators showing the booking status:

      • Hollow dots – Checked out
      • Blue dots – In-house
      • Black dots – Upcoming booking
      • White/grey dots – Cancelled booking

      This helps you quickly see if a guest is due to arrive, currently staying, or already checked out.

      Rooms without Goki / Static Codes

      Remember:

      • In Surry Hills, all rooms do not have Goki except Room 3 and Room 14. These rooms use static codes, which you can find in the property info section in Notion.
      • In Potts Point, Room 105 also does not have Goki and uses a static code, also listed in the property info section in Notion.
      Closing

      That’s the basic navigation of Goki for reception.

      As a receptionist, your main tasks in Goki are:

      • Checking bookings and guest details
      • Generating digital keys
      • Adjusting access when bookings change
      • Making sure guests can enter the property and their room smoothly.

      If you’re unsure about anything, always check with your senior receptionist or manager before making big changes.

      3. Go High Level (GHL)

      GHL Training – System Overview

      GHL is our omni-inbox system.

      It consolidates emails, SMS, WhatsApp messages, and calls into one place, so we don’t have to jump between different apps.

      We use GHL to:

      • Manage guest check-ins
      • Send automated and manual communications to guests
      Navigating the GHL Dashboard

      When you log in to GHL, you’ll see the main dashboard.

      On the left-hand side, you’ll see the menu, including:

      • Conversations – this is our omni-inbox
      • Opportunities – where we track check-in flow and other pipelines
      • Other tools we may use depending on our role

      If you’re unsure about anything, always check with your senior receptionist or manager before making big changes.

      Conversations

      Most of the time as a receptionist, you’ll work inside Conversations.

      Click on Conversations on the left.

      Here you can see:

      • SMS, emails, and sometimes missed call logs
      • Conversations are usually sorted with the most recent at the top.

      To send or reply to a message:

      • Click on the conversation on the left side
      • Type your reply in the message box at the bottom
      • Then click Send

      If a guest directly asks to be added to the dinner list, we will manually add them to the list and add a dinner tag to their contact details in GHL so they are properly counted.

      Always:

      • Check the guest name and property before replying
      • Keep replies clear, polite, and professional
      • Make sure to respond to messages once you open them, so nothing is left unattended. It is mandatory to monitor messages throughout your shift.
      Making Calls

      GHL also allows us to make calls directly from the system.

      To make a call:

      • Go to the top right of the screen
      • Click the green phone icon
      • You can enter the phone number directly, or click Contacts and search for the guest name

      Once you select the contact, you can start the call.

      Next, let’s look at check-in management using GHL.

      If the guest hasn’t completed their check-in, or they lost the link, you can resend the registration form manually from GHL.

      Here’s how:

      • Go back to Conversations
      • Open the chat with that guest
      • At the bottom of the chat, click the plus (+) button
      • In the search bar, type the word “link”

      • Select the custom value called “Digital check-in link”

      This will insert the correct check-in link into the message.

      Then send the message so the guest can complete the form.

      Closing

      That’s a basic overview of using GHL (Go High Level) as a receptionist.

      We covered:

      • Using the omni-inbox for messagesUsing the omni-inbox for messages
      • Making calls
      • Managing check-ins and resending the digital check-in link
      • Handling dinner reservations and creating the final list for staff

      If you’re ever unsure, ask a senior receptionist or your manager before changing anything important.

      6. Ring

      Introduction to Ring (Property Security)

      Ring is a web platform that connects to cameras, doorbells, and other security devices at your properties. Staff can watch live video feeds, review recorded events, and respond to guest access requests—all from a web browser.

      It’s perfect for seeing who’s at your property, speaking directly to visitors, and managing entry without being physically present

      Here are the things we can do with Ring:

      • Live video monitoring
      • Review recorded events
      • Two-way communication with guests
      • Manage entry remotely
      Getting Started with Ring

      Here is how you get started with Spaces

      • Step 1: Access the Website
        • Open your web browser and go to ring.com. Click ‘Log In’ at the top right, enter your Ring account credentials, and complete 2FA if prompted.
      • Step 2: Explore the Sidebar
        • Once logged in, the sidebar on the left displays all your Ring devices—doorbells, cameras, and security lights. You can navigate between properties or devices to see what’s happening in real time.
      Using Ring Features

      With Ring, you can:

      • View Live Video: Watch cameras in real time to see activity at your property.
      • Monitor Property & Recorded Events: Review past motion alerts, doorbell rings, and recordings.
      • Check Guest Assistance Requests: See which guests need help with entry.
      • Two-Way Communication: Speak directly to guests through the mic, and hear their responses in real time.
      Quick Tips for Ring

      To get the best experience:

      • Use Ring on a larger screen for easier video review.
      • Refresh your browser occasionally if devices disconnect.
      • Never share your login to keep your properties secure.
      Closing

      And that’s your introduction to Ring! By using this tool, you can monitor your properties, communicate with guests instantly, and enhance security across the board. Together with Spaces, these platforms make property management safer and more efficient than ever.

      7. DMSS

      Introduction to DMSS (CCTV Monitoring)

      DMSS is a mobile CCTV application used to monitor security cameras across multiple properties. Similar to Ring, it allows staff to view live video feeds and review recordings. However, DMSS is designed to handle a larger number of CCTV cameras, making it ideal for properties with extensive camera coverage.

      Here are the things we can do with DMSS:

      • Live CCTV monitoring
      • access multiple cameras per property
      • Real-time visibility from your phone
      • Centralized security monitoring
      Getting Started with DMSS

      Here is how you get started with Spaces

      • Step 1: Open your App Store or Google Play Store
        • Search for DMSS, and download the app to your phone.
      • Step 2: Log In
        • Once installed, open the app and log in using the provided DMSS account credentials. If required, complete any verification steps to secure your access.
      Navigating the App and Viewing Cameras

      When you log in, you’ll immediately see access to all CCTV cameras linked to your account. Cameras are organized by property, making it easy to switch between locations.

      Unlike other platforms, DMSS allows you to view multiple cameras at once. You can tap on any camera to expand it to full screen and monitor activity in real time.

      This setup gives staff instant visibility across entrances, hallways, common areas, and other monitored zones—directly from their phone.

      Monitoring and Security Features

      With DMSS, you can:

      • View live CCTV footage in real time
      • Switch between cameras quickly
      • Monitor multiple areas across different properties
      • Stay aware of on-site activity without being physically present
      Quick Tips for Using DMSS

      Here are a few tips to get the most out of DMSS:

      • Use a stable internet connection for smooth live viewing
      • Familiarize yourself with camera locations per property
      • Regularly check cameras during peak hours
      • Keep your login details secure and never share access
      Closing

      And that’s a quick overview of DMSS. This app gives you instant access to all CCTV cameras across your properties, helping you maintain safety, awareness, and control at all times. When used alongside Spaces and Ring, DMSS completes a powerful set of tools for efficient and secure property management.
