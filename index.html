      1. About Azzurro Hotels

      Azzurro Hotels operates modern pod hostels and serviced accommodation across Australia. Our properties are designed for efficient, contactless stays, using technology and automation rather than traditional onsite reception desks.

      We operate using a self check-in (Airbnb-style) model, where:

      • Guests check in online before arrival
      • Access is provided via secure door codes
      • There is no physical front desk
      • Guest support is provided remotely by our virtual reception team

      This model allows us to offer flexibility to guests while maintaining consistent service standards across all properties.

      3. Our Operating Model (How Things Work)

      Key Principles

      • 24-hour self check-in for guests
      • Check-in time starts from 3:00 PM

      1. Cloudbeds

      Cloudbeds Training – System Overview

      Cloudbeds is our main central system.

      This is where all bookings from different channels, such as Booking.com and Agoda, are collected and managed in one place.

      Dashboard Overview

      Upon logging into Cloudbeds, this is the Dashboard.

      This page gives you a quick overview of:

      • Arrivals and departures
      • Current accommodations booked
      • Today’s activities
      • A list of guest reservations

      This helps you immediately see what’s happening for the day.

      Switching Properties

      To access different properties, click the property name located at the upper left corner of the page.

      A dropdown will appear showing all other available properties. Simply select the property you want to manage.

      The three horizontal bars beside the property name open the main menu, where you can access other Cloudbeds features and options.

      Calendar View

      On the upper right of the page, click the calendar icon to open the Cloudbeds Calendar.

      This is the most commonly used page during your shift.

      Since all bookings come from multiple channels, any availability shown here is automatically reflected across all connected booking platforms.

      The calendar uses color indicators to show reservation status:

      • Blue – Confirmed bookings
      • Green – Checked-in guests
      • Gray – Checked-out guests

      You can click the legend at the bottom left for reference.

      Viewing a Reservation

      When you click on a reservation, you’ll see full details including:

      • Guest name
      • Reservation status (confirmed, in-house, or checked-out)
      • Number of guests
      • Balance due
      • Reservation notes

      Always check the notes section for important instructions or reminders.

      Room Types and Rooms (Left Panel)

      On the left side, you’ll see the Room Types and individual room setup.

      Guests book a room type, and Cloudbeds automatically assigns one of the rooms under that category based on availability.

      Reservations List & Filter Page

      On the upper right, click the Reservations icon to open the Reservations List & Filter page.

      Here’s what you can do on this page:

      • Filter section (top):
        • Narrow reservations by booking date, stay dates, status, room type, booking source/channel, and meal plan.
      • Apply / Clear:
        • Apply your selected filters or reset them.
      • Reservations table (bottom):
        • Displays reservation number, guest name, booking date, property, room number, and check-in date.
      • Search bar:
        • Quickly find a reservation by name, reservation number, or keyword.
      • Bulk Action:
        • Perform actions on multiple reservations at once.
      • Pagination & view size:Pagination & view size:
        • Control how many reservations appear per page and navigate through results.

      This page is mainly used to search, review, filter, and manage reservations efficiently.

      Top-Right Utility Icons

      Finally, on the top-right corner, you’ll see the utility icons:

      • Bell icon – Notifications for reservations, tasks, and system alerts
      • Megaphone icon – Important Cloudbeds announcements and updates
      • Printer icon – Print tools for reservation lists and reports
      • Chat bubble icon – Messages or Inbox for guest and channel communications
      • User icon – Profile settings, preferences, permissions, and logout
      Closing Line

      Cloudbeds is the main system you’ll use throughout your shift, so becoming familiar with the dashboard, calendar, and reservations page is essential for smooth daily operations.

      2. Goki

      GOKI Training – System Overview

      Goki is our digital key system.

      It allows us to enable and disable access to rooms and entrances remotely. Goki is connected to CloudBeds, so once a booking is in the system, guests can access their rooms and entrances using a PIN code or the Goki app, depending on the setup. Guests who download the Goki app and activate their key can also open the doors directly from the app. When they are near the main entrance or their room, they can tap the digital key button in the app to unlock the door. Their access will only work during their stay dates and will automatically stop after check-out.

      Dashboard Overview

      First, open Goki in your browser and log in using your work email and password. After you log in, you’ll see the dashboard.

      Here, you can see:

      • Today’s check-ins and check-outs
      • Current in-house guests

        • and

      • Lock/battery status for rooms
        • Ideally, batteries lower than 40% should be changed to avoid access issues for guests.

      On the top left, you can change the property by clicking the dropdown and selecting the correct address. Always make sure you’re on the right property before checking reservations or sending keys.

      Messages

      Next, let’s look at Messages.

      • Messages allow us to communicate directly with guests through Goki.
      • Guests can only receive and send messages if they have the Goki app and an account.
      • If a guest does not have the app or an account, Goki will not give you the option to message them.

        • Unread Goki messages are marked in red.

      • Once a message is opened, it will lose the red mark.
      • Always make sure to respond to messages once you open them, so nothing is left unattended. It is mandatory to monitor messages throughout your shift.
      Shoutouts

      Shoutoutis Goki’s message blast feature.

      • We use Shoutouts to inform guests in bulk, like a notice board.
      • You can send mass messagesv to:
        • All guests in the property
        • Guests in specific rooms
        • Guests in a specific property only

      This is useful for announcements like quiet hours, maintenance notices, or events.

      Activities

      Under Activities, we post any activities happening in the property and invite guests to sign up.

      A common example is our complimentary dinner sign-up.

      • We create an activity in Goki.
      • Guests can sign up through the app.
      • Those who sign up will appear in the list.

      By around 4 p.m., we check the list and make sure everyone who signed up is added to the system or prepared for the activity.

      Reservations

      Now, let’s go to the Reservations section.

      Here, you can:

      • Check guest reservation details
      • See the booking status
      • View their PIN code for Goki access.

      You’ll also see dot indicators showing the booking status:

      • Hollow dots – Checked out
      • Blue dots – In-house
      • Black dots – Upcoming booking
      • White/grey dots – Cancelled booking

      This helps you quickly see if a guest is due to arrive, currently staying, or already checked out.

      Rooms without Goki / Static Codes

      Remember:

      • In Surry Hills, all rooms do not have Goki except Room 3 and Room 14. These rooms use static codes, which you can find in the property info section in Notion.
      • In Potts Point, Room 105 also does not have Goki and uses a static code, also listed in the property info section in Notion.
      Closing

      That’s the basic navigation of Goki for reception.

      As a receptionist, your main tasks in Goki are:

      • Checking bookings and guest details
      • Generating digital keys
      • Adjusting access when bookings change
      • Making sure guests can enter the property and their room smoothly.

      If you’re unsure about anything, always check with your senior receptionist or manager before making big changes.

      Check-In & Check-Out Process

      Introduction

      Hi team. We’ll do the full check-in process using Cloudbeds and GoKi. Cloudbeds is our central system that collects bookings from all channels and we’ll spend most of our time on the Calendar, because that’s where we manage arrivals, room assignments, and check-in status.

      Before checking anyone in, make sure you’re in the correct property. Cloudbeds supports multiple properties, so you always confirm you’re viewing the right one.

      Action:

      • Click the property name at the upper-left.
      • Select the correct property from the dropdown.
      • Click the Calendar icon to open the Calendar page.
      Step 1: Find Today’s Arrivals (Calendar Date + Visual Status Feature)

      On the Calendar, Cloudbeds highlights the current date at the top, which makes it easy to identify today’s check-ins and check-outs.

      You’ll also notice the calendar is color-coded.

      Action:

      • Go to today’s highlighted date.
      • Locate the guest you will check in (typically shown in blue if not yet checked in).
      Step 2: Open the Reservation Card

      Now we click the booking block to open the reservation card. This is your quick scan before you go deeper.

      Action:

      • Click the guest booking on the calendar.

      On this reservation card, Cloudbeds shows key details like:

      • Guest name
      • Booking status (e.g., confirmed)
      • Booking reference
      • Booking source (sometimes shown beside the reference)
      • Check-in/check-out dates
      • Number of guests
      • Estimated arrival time
      • Grand total
      • Balance due, if any

      This quick scan tells us if the booking looks normal before we proceed.

      Interpret Dots (Notes + Balance Due Indicators)

      Cloudbeds also uses dot indicators on the calendar bookings:

      • A yellow dot means there are notes on the booking.
      • A red dot means there is a balance due showing in the system.

      These dots are important because notes can include guest requests or payment instructions, and balance due affects whether we can check them in.

      • If you see a yellow dot, you’ll review Notes later in Reservation Details
      • If you see a red dot, you must verify the balance due in Reservation Details before check-in.
      Step 3: Open Reservation Details (Go to Reservation Feature)

      Next, we open Reservation Details, because that’s where we verify the guest requirements, ID, payments, and notes.

      Action:

      • Click Go to Reservation

        • (or “Check in & Go to Reservation” only if you are sure everything is ready; we’ll do verification first.)

      Step 4: Verify Check-in Requirements (Documents Tab Feature)

      Inside Reservation Details, we go straight to the Documents tab. This tab shows whether the guest has completed our required check-in documents.

      Action:

      • Click Documents tab.

        • In our process, the two main requirements are:

      • the Registration form
      • a Valid Photo ID

        • If the guest has completed them, they will appear here. If the guest hasn’t arrived yet, the tab may still be empty.

      Decision:

      • If complete - proceed
      • If incomplete - send reminder

      IF the requirements are incomplete, we send the guest a reminder to complete the form and upload their ID. If they arrive without completing them, they must complete these at reception before we check them in.

      Step 5: Verify and Upload Photo ID (Guest Details Tab Feature)

      Now we verify the ID. Cloudbeds stores the uploaded ID under Guest Details, so this is where we confirm and upload it.

      Action:

      • Click Guest Details tab.

      Here you can see the guest contact details like email and phone number, and this is also where we upload the passport or photo ID. For passports, make sure the photo and details are clear. IDs must be valid, no expired documents.

      Action:

      • Upload the ID/passport image in this tab (if not already uploaded or if you received it separately).
      Step 6: Check Notes (Notes Tab + Yellow Dot Feature)

      Next, we review Notes. Notes are shared across reception, finance, and management, so anyone can add important instructions here.

      Action:

      • Click Notes tab.

      Notes may include:

      • guest requests (example: lower bunk request)
      • operational info (example: do not move)
      • borrowed item tracking
      • payment instructions from Finance (example: Expedia VCC paid)

      Always check the date of notes because returning guests may have older notes that are no longer relevant.

      Action:

      • If there’s a special request, keep it in mind for room assignment decisions.
      • Add notes if needed (example: “Above 50 – prioritize bottom bunk”).
      Step 7: Verify Balance Due (Red Dot Handling + Reservation Details Balance Check)

      Now we check payments. If you saw a red dot on the calendar, it means Cloudbeds is showing a balance due, but we must verify if it’s accurate.

      Action:

      • In Reservation Details, look at the Balance Due shown in the booking summary area.

      Sometimes the calendar still shows a red dot even if the guest has already paid. If Reservation Details shows the balance due is 0.00, then the guest has paid and it may just be a system delay.

      Expedia rule (Channel Collect)

      Also, be mindful of Expedia bookings. Expedia payments can appear as balance due because Expedia releases payment later, even though the guest already paid them.

      Action:

      • Confirm booking source/payment type indicates Expedia/channel collect.
      • Check Notes for “Expedia VCC paid” when available.

      Decision:

      • If balance is truly due (not zero and not an approved channel-collect exception) - do not check in
      • Proceed with sending a payment link instead. Go to Folio - Add Payment - set Payment Type to Pay by Link - select the charge to collect - set Link expires after to 1 day - Continue. Copy the generated payment link and paste it in the Notes section, and you may also send it to the guest.
      • If balance is zero or Expedia paid via channel collect - proceed

      Room Moves, Locks, and Special Handling (Calendar Management Features)

      Sometimes we need to move a guest to another room/bed. Cloudbeds allows this through room assignment controls.

      Unassign / Quick Edit

      • Unassign Room removes the current assignment so you can move them.
      • Quick Edit lets you adjust room assignment, dates, or split bookings.

      Locked bookings (Padlock icon)

      If a booking has a padlock icon, that means it’s locked, usually due to a request like ‘do not move.’ Locked bookings cannot be dragged.

      Rule:

      “If you lock a booking, always leave a note explaining why.”

      Female-only rooms check

      For female-only rooms, all guests must be female. If a male booking appears, you must move it and escalate as needed.

      Check-Out Process

      All guests must check out at reception before leaving, so confirm their name or room and bed number and note if they booked via Booking.com (BDC) or another platform. Ask if they’ve checked their belongings (bed area, locker, and common spaces), and ensure any borrowed items (e.g., hair dryer, containers) are returned to reception or left on the bed so they won’t be charged a penalty. If they had a good stay, politely ask for a review and to mention your name, show the QR/send the link, and if they booked via BDC, remind them they may receive a review email within 24–48 hours. Thank them, wish them safe travels, and invite them back.
